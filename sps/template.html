Editor's Note


Katie Cartee


Seed has grown so much, I almost can’t believe that we started out with less than ten people and no class period just a couple of short years ago. It’s absolutely amazing how far we’ve come. I feel like each issue gets better and better. This semester alone we’ve had so many new ideas and people. Christopher’s website is new and up and running, meaning that if you want to view the magazine with uninterrupted scrolling and a wonderful layout, you can read it there. I’d like to welcome and thank all our new staff members, for learning so quickly and working hard to make this issue the best we could. I don’t think I’ve ever worked with a group of people, both new and those who are returning from last year, who work so well together. I’d go crazy without them. I think this was the smoothest magazine-creation process we’ve had. This issue happened without any major problems, mostly because of that harmony. Crunch week is always tough but they handled it like pros and deserve all the thanks you and I can give them.

I’d like to talk about the people who submitted things, too. I know I’m at risk of sounding sappy here, but I appreciate every single piece we get. Keep ‘em coming, please! Everything you send is enjoyed and discussed by the Seed staff. Also, a side note: if you don’t see a piece of your work in this issue, but you did submit something, it’s not because we hated it or you. We’ve already begun stockpiling things for spring and you’ll probably see it in the next issue.

Enjoy the magazine and I hope to see you all in spring!
